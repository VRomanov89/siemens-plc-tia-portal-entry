# Section 6 — Testing, Hardware Connection, and Capstone: Introduction

**Estimated segment duration:** 3–5 minutes
**Type:** Section orientation (structural — not counted in lesson totals)

---

## What You'll Learn in This Section

This is the final section — and the one that separates this course from every simulation-only alternative on the market.

Three lessons:

1. **PLCSIM, online monitoring, watch tables, and the diagnostics buffer** — TIA Portal's testing toolkit in depth. You'll use all of it to verify behavior, force inputs, catch faults, and read what the CPU recorded when something went wrong. These tools apply equally to PLCSIM and real hardware.

2. **Connecting to a physical S7-1200** — the step most online courses skip entirely. IP address assignment on a new out-of-box PLC, PG/PC interface configuration, the download dialog explained, and a complete troubleshooting guide for the most common connection failures. If you have hardware, this is where it gets real.

3. **Capstone: Conveyor control system** — a complete industrial PLC project built from a problem statement. No instructor scaffolding. A real-world scenario, a full set of requirements, and success criteria you can verify objectively. This is the deliverable that proves course completion.

---

## Who This Section Is For

**If you have a physical S7-1200:** Do Lesson 6.1, then Lesson 6.2 to get the PLC on the network, then use both PLCSIM and hardware to validate the capstone.

**If you're working in simulation only:** Lesson 6.2 is still worth watching — you'll encounter a physical PLC at some point in your career, and the connection workflow will be familiar when you do. The capstone is fully completable in PLCSIM with watch table verification as an accepted alternative.
