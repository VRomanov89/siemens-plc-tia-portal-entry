# Section 1 — The Siemens Platform: Conclusion

**Estimated segment duration:** 3–5 minutes

## What You Covered in This Section

Two lessons in, and you already know more than most beginners do when they open TIA Portal for the first time.

**From Lesson 1.1:**
- A PLC is a ruggedized industrial computer built for deterministic, real-time control — not a general-purpose machine
- The scan cycle (read inputs → execute program → write outputs → repeat) is the fundamental operating loop — every timing behavior you'll encounter in programming traces back to this
- TIA Portal is the engineering software; the PLC firmware is separate — they must be version-compatible

**From Lesson 1.2:**
- The S7-1200 (G1 and G2) is the right starting point — compact, cost-effective, appropriate for the vast majority of beginner and small-to-medium applications
- The G2 is a 2024 hardware revision: different physical dimensions, incompatible signal modules — always check the nameplate before ordering accessories
- The S7-1500 is not a "better S7-1200" — it's a different product for larger applications with more complex requirements
- You have a complete checklist of what you need before Section 2
