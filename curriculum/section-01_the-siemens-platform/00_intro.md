# Section 1 — The Siemens Platform: Introduction

**Estimated segment duration:** 3–5 minutes
**Type:** Section orientation (structural — not counted in lesson totals)

---

## What You'll Learn in This Section

Welcome to the course. Before writing a single line of ladder logic, you need to know what you're working with — what a PLC actually is, how it thinks, and which piece of Siemens hardware you should be using.

In this section you'll cover two things:

1. **What a PLC is and how it works** — the core mental model that underpins every lesson that follows. You'll understand the scan cycle, why PLCs are built differently from regular computers, and why Siemens TIA Portal is a skill worth building.

2. **The Siemens hardware landscape** — the S7-1200, the S7-1200 G2 (released 2024, and different enough to matter), and the S7-1500. You'll leave with a clear answer to "which one should I buy?" and a complete checklist of what you need to follow the rest of this course.

This section is deliberately short. The goal is to give you the decision framework and mental model you need before touching any software — not to dump a hardware spec sheet on you.

---

## Why This Section Exists

Most courses skip straight to TIA Portal. That creates two problems: learners don't know which hardware they're looking at, and they don't understand why the PLC behaves the way it does when they start programming. This section closes both gaps in under 30 minutes.
